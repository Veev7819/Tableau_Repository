##  Overview
This dashboard analyzes employee attrition patterns to help HR teams understand **who is leaving, from where, and at what stage of employment**.  
The insights support proactive **retention planning and workforce stability**.

## Workforce Summary
- **Total Employees:** 1,485  
- **Employees Who Left:** 243  
- **Attrition Rate:** **16%**  
- **Gender Breakdown of Attrition:**
  - **Male:** 156 employees
  - **Female:** 87 employees

## Age Distribution
- The majority of employees fall within the **25–44 age group**
- This represents the organization’s **core working population**, making retention in this segment especially critical

## Department-Level Attrition
- **Research & Development (R&D)** experienced the **highest attrition**
  - **139 employees lost**
- This suggests potential challenges related to:
  - workload
  - role expectations
  - career progression

## Role with Highest Churn
- **Lab Technicians** recorded the highest number of departures
- Indicates a need to reassess:
  - job satisfaction
  - compensation
  - career development pathways

## Tenure Analysis
- A significant portion of employee attrition occurs **after 2 years of employment**
- This highlights a **critical retention window** where targeted interventions could have the greatest impact

## Tools & Techniques
- Tableau Desktop
- Calculated fields & filters
- Attrition and tenure analysis
- Department and role-based segmentation

## Key Takeaways
- With these insights, HR teams can:
- Identify high-risk departments and roles
- Focus retention strategies around the 2-year tenure milestone
- Improve workforce planning and reduce costly turnover

## Use Cases
- HR analytics and reporting
- Employee retention strategy
- Workforce planning
- Executive decision support
